# STAFFD Voice & Vocabulary

> Single source of truth for how STAFFD talks to users. Any new copy must
> match this guide. The product name is a verb: you **STAFF** your business.

---

## The frame

- **You** are the owner / employer
- **STAFFD** is the hiring platform
- **Specialists** are your employees on call
- **Departments** are your org chart
- **The CEO** is your strategic advisor (capitalized as a role)

## Word choices

### ✅ Use these

| Concept | Word |
|---|---|
| Your workforce | **staff** |
| Individual workers | **specialists** |
| Organizational unit | **department** |
| Specialist's deliverable | **work**, **drafts**, **reports** |
| Active subscription | **on payroll**, **on staff** |
| Action of subscribing | **hire**, **staff up** |
| Plan upgrade | **promote**, **add to payroll** |
| Subscription tier | **plan** (secondary, "what you've staffed" primary) |

### ❌ Avoid these

| Don't say | Why |
|---|---|
| "AI team" | Brand kills with software framing |
| "AI agent" | We sell specialists, not agents |
| "AI generates" | Specialists *write*, *draft*, *produce* |
| "AI-powered" | Drop it entirely |
| "Subscribe" | We **hire** |
| "Buy a plan" | We **staff** the business |
| "Bots" | Never |
| "Output" | "Work" or "drafts" |
| "Run agent" | "Put a specialist to work" |

### When "team" is OK

- Inside a single department: "The Marketing team" reads naturally
- Cross-department references: "across every team" (CEO context)
- For variety occasionally — but default to **staff**

---

## Tone

- **Direct.** Like a sharp chief of staff giving a brief.
- **Confident.** No hedging, no "we believe", no "AI-powered."
- **Specific.** Specialists have names and roles, not capabilities.
- **Owner-respectful.** The user is the boss. Talk to them like one.

## Copy patterns

| Surface | Pattern |
|---|---|
| Headlines | "**Staff** your X." or "Hire the X you can't afford yet." |
| CTAs | "Hire Starter staff", "Promote to Pro", "Add a department" |
| Loading states | "Drafting...", "Writing...", "Preparing your briefing..." |
| Empty states | "Your staff is on call. Open a department to put a specialist to work." |
| Welcome | "Welcome back. Your staff is on duty." |

## Examples — before / after

| Before | After |
|---|---|
| "Your AI team is ready" | "Your staff is on duty" |
| "Run an agent" | "Put a specialist to work" |
| "Subscribe to Growth" | "Hire Growth staff" |
| "AI-generated content" | "Work produced by your team" |
| "Upgrade to Pro" | "Promote to Pro" |
| "STAFFD — Your AI-Powered Business" | "STAFFD — Hire your business team" |

---

When in doubt, ask: *would a CEO say it this way about their actual employees?*
If yes, keep it. If it sounds like a SaaS dashboard, rewrite it.
